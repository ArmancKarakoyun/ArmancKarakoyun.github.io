ABOUT ME

Hello there! I received B.S. degrees in Mechanical Engineering & Energy Systems Engineering (double major program) at Istanbul Bilgi University in 2018 and I was granted the Award of Best Senior Project in Mechanical Engineering. During my undergraduate years, I had the opportunity to work in many positions within the university, from librarian to assistant student. As a student assistant, I helped lecturers in the laboratory courses of thermodynamics I, Physics I, and Computer-Aided Machine Design courses and contributed to the progress of the courses. In addition, from 2017 to 2018, I worked as a student researcher in a Scientific and Technological Research Council of Turkey (TUBITAK)-funded project named "Nanostructured Multilayered Thermoelectric Materials". I was able to enter the Research Center for Hydrogen and Fuel Cells as an intern at the Korea Institute of Science and Technology (KIST), South Korea's most prestigious institute, thanks to the laboratory skills and abilities I obtained during my undergraduate studies.

After the internship, I realized that I had lost interest in working on this subject. I wanted to pursue a career in robotics, so I applied for a master's degree in AI & Robotics at the University of Science and Technology. I`m currently a master's student in the field of AI & Robotics Engineering, as well as I am engaged in several project-based research work within KIST since August 2020. I am working as a Junior Mechanical Design Engineer in Dr. Kim Chunwoo`s research team. We are working on "Development of the Stereo X-ray and 3D Endoscope Guided Spine Surgery Robot System". Our goal is to develop a robot system for hard tissue surgery that can achieve increased surgical accuracy and safety, shortened surgery time, and reduced medical staff fatigue and radiation exposure by introducing a teleoperated surgical robot system for minimally invasive spinal canal stenosis and spinal deformity correction surgery. My duty in the team entails designing, developing, and prototyping the surgical robot instruments while keeping design constraints in mind.
